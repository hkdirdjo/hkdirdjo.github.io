3D Printing Station

To expand my FDM capabilities beyond my current Prusa Mini, I am building a 350mm Voron Trident which will integrate the upcoming Bondtech INDX multi-material system. I looked online for an off-the-shelf solution that would house both printers in a compact footprint, but when I couldn't find anything suitable, I decided to build my own! The key features of the table are as follows:
- Custom size and height shelves with room for an enclosed Prusa Mini
- 3030/3060 Aluminium extrusion frame with Loctite'd steel load-bearing connectors
- 1/4" laser cut plywood bracing to reduce resonance
- High-back intended to connect to the Voron Trident frame to provide additional dampening at the height of the CoreXY assembly. The high back will also support a platform for supporting multiple spools.

This project was a great learning experience with regard to the capabilities and tolerances of laser cutters. The Prusa Mini is already puttering away happily, and I'll be excited to install the Voron Trident in December!
